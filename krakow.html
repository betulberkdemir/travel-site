POLAND | Krakow

Discover Krakow's Historic Treasures

Krakow, a charming city in southern Poland, is known for its rich history, vibrant culture , and lively student atmosphere. Begin your journey in the historic Old Town, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, where you can explore the stunning Wawel Castle and the majestic St. Mary's Basilica. Stroll through the Main Market Square, one of the largest medieval squares in Europe, and visit the Cloth Hall, a Renaissance trading outpost filled with local crafts and souvenirs. Even you can find the building from "Schindler's list" film. It's a film made from real history so you can watch the film before visiting Krakow. You can see the building on second photo.


Experience Krakow's Lively Atmosphere and Nightlife

Krakow is a small but bustling city with a large student population, making it a lively and energetic place to visit. The city's cheap public transportation, including trams and buses, makes it easy to get around and explore all it has to offer. For a poignant historical experience, take a day trip to the Auschwitz-Birkenau concentration camp, located a bit outside the city. You can reach it by bus or private car, and the visit provides a sobering reminder of the atrocities of World War II. In the evenings, Krakow comes alive with an active nightlife scene. The city is filled with bars, clubs, and live music venues that cater to a variety of tastes. One of the popular spots is Forum, a major meeting point where locals and tourists gather. This trendy area is home to numerous cafes and restaurants, offering a perfect place to relax and enjoy the vibrant atmosphere.



Indulge in Krakow's Culinary Delights and Cultural Offerings

Krakow is also known for its delicious cuisine. Be sure to try traditional Polish dishes like pierogi (dumplings), bigos (hunter's stew), and oscypek (smoked cheese). The city's many restaurants and street vendors provide ample opportunities to savor these local flavors. For accommodations, consider staying at Atlantis Hostel, a budget-friendly option that offers great value for money. Located conveniently, this hostel provides comfortable lodgings and a friendly atmosphere, ideal for travelers looking to make the most of their stay in Krakow.


Don't miss a visit to Kazimierz, the historic Jewish quarter, which is now a trendy area filled with cafes, art galleries, and cultural sites.
